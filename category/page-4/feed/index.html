We bring few celebs experience from memory lane. Let’s check what they have to share.

Nandish Sandhu: 

Makar Sankranti used be like this huge festival in Rajasthan. We used to celebrate Lohri and Makar Sankranti both with so much enthusiasm. I remember dad getting Gajak, Revdi and Til Barfi and mom used to make Til ladoo, pheeni and gajar Ka halwa. The fridge used to be filled with sweets. I remember mom giving small gifts to newly married couples. In the evening we had a huge bonfire where all friends and family would gather and sing songs.
To keep the tradition going this year I’ve brought Revdi and will have some sweet cooked with jaggery for friends and family and have a small family dinner at home. Making a small donation from mom’s side for some girls education.

Anuj Sachdeva:

My kite flying memories are from my delhi days when I would enjoy the festival with friends and family. The best memory is every year going to the temple on the day and distributing and donating food to the needy. My mom reminds me to do this every year And I feel it’s a good habit. And yes she makes sure that we are complete vegetarians and don’t have eggs even on this auspicious day.

Shantanu Maheshwari: 
When I was small all I used to do was see my brothers fly kites & I use to request them to allow me to hold the latie, coz by doing that I use to feel I will at least be involved in the process of kite flying …& Use to enjoy doing this. Now I have kind of stopped flying kites coz it proves fatal to animals specially birds & I don’t like that … Its risky for motorists also .. So I kind of gradually stopped celebrating makar sankranti by kite flying instead I eat good food.

Shubhangi Latkar:

 I remember this festival from the days of the 1st year of my marriage when my mother in law bought beautiful black sarre that year for me.  It was with so much love and care and respect.  This year the climate is really pleasant to enjoy the festival along with sweets made of feel and gaggery.  


Rohan Mehra:

In Punjab its celebrated as Lohri…its always a get together scene at my place in Amritsar…So all the family members and friends come over, fly kites, dine together and have a bonfire scene because its always too cold in Punjab.  So we are planning to the same this year as well

Gaurav Sharma: 
As well all know , makar sankranti is a festival of kites and happiness .. My memory of childhood consists same , as almost everyone .. Me and brother saurabh made some of best memories of the festival .. On the terrace of my house in meerut , Playing music on the transistor as it was the trend that time , we flew kites my father got for us .. My mom made khichdi for all of us as tradition in sankranti .. But the best fun was to collect kites jo kat ke aati thi ..
 So wish that time culd be back , As i see not much happening in mumbai now in this festival , today I spent my day distributing sweets on the sets as im shooting with my sweet memories of the festival.. And i wish all a safe sankranti , specially when on terraces. . God bless you all .. cheers ..

]]> http://livepic.in/justshowbiz/memories-from-makarsankranti/feed/ 0 Uttaran draws to an end today http://livepic.in/justshowbiz/uttaran-draws-to-an-end-today/ http://livepic.in/justshowbiz/uttaran-draws-to-an-end-today/#comments Wed, 21 Jan 2015 11:27:16 +0000 http://livepic.in/justshowbiz/?p=71

It is said that all good things must come to an end. After entertaining viewers over a span of more than 6 years, COLORS’  flagship show, Uttaran is drawing its curtains today. Today last episode of Uttran will be telecasted.

The show, which  kicked off as a journey of two friends, Ichcha and Tapasya belonging to two different socio-economic classes of the society raked in some strong feedback from the viewers which made it one of the top-rated shows on Indian television. Even now, as the show’s closure is imminent, Uttaran will always be remembered as one of the most phenomenal shows in the television industry which has had a positive impact on the lives of its artists and viewers.

Today, the story of Uttaran has come a full circle with the journey reaching a junction which concentrates on Meethi and Mukta‘s equation the young daughters of Ichcha and Tapasya. Reminiscing their times spent during the shoot, the actors – who have contributed towards making Uttaran a truly sensational television offering – have been  reliving some of their most cherished moments from the years gone by.

Tina Dutta, whose has portrayed a wide range of characters on the show and today, essays the role of Meethi on the show, exclaims, “My experience of working on Uttaran can only be described as awesome! Uttaran was my debut in a lead role on television because of which the show will always remain very close to my heart. Not only have I learnt  a lot from all the senior actors that I have worked with, but I have also grown as a professional in many ways.” When further quizzed about what she will miss most about the show, Tina is quick to respond, “Undoubtedly, I will miss all the fun that I have with my costars – especially Mrunal (Akash), Sreejita (Mukta). Pratima Ma’am (Nani) and the entire team of the show.”

Sreejita De, who essays the role of Mukta in the show, looks back at her Uttaran days fondly. She said, “Every day has been a fresh memory while working on Uttaran. But yes, if there is one particular day I remember vividly, it has to be my first day on the shoot when I met the cast and crew of the show! The team welcomed me with open arms and there has been no looking back for me since then. I will miss meeting everyone on a daily basis.”

Admitting that the news of Uttaran coming to an end has made him enjoy the small moments more than ever before, Mrunal Jain who portrays the role of Akash on the show says,  “During my Uttaran stint, I bonded with my cast and crew like a family. We have spent more than 10 hours  together on a daily basis and it was natural for us to foster the strong relationships that we have. Speaking about how they all love to work as a unit, Mrunal further adds, “I love it when our producers come over for lunch every day. We are going to work on New Year’s Eve as well as  1st January and ring in the New Year on the set!”Signing off with a vote of thanks for his fans, Mrunal concludes saying, “It’s because of the love that our fans have showered on us that we have been able to complete these 6 years successfully. I want our fans to know that we will always be around to entertain them especially because their support has helped us reach where we are today!”

Rashami Desai, who has been an integral part of Uttaran through its various phases, summarizes her journey with Uttaran by saying, “My journey in Uttaran has been exceptionally memorable. The show has allowed me to grow as an actor. Having portrayed the various roles of Tapasya – the daughter, the wife and the mother – has helped me gain more perspective as a professional. Of course, the bond between Tapasya and Ichcha has been etched in the history of Hindi GEC shows. I am grateful to my fans and viewers who have given me a popular status in spite of Tapasya’s grey character. Looking back, I feel overwhelmed at how the show never let the ball drop and kept the momentum going.”

Talking about his special association with Uttaran, Gaurav Chopraa said “Uttaran is the show with which I have been associated for the longest time.  Through the show, I have experienced a journey that I will never forget because it has taught me many things. It has been a legend in the world of television not only because of its long run but also because of the kind of adulation it has received consistently from the audience. Uttaran, I believe, is more popular than most shows on television today and that is something which cannot be gauged through the show’s ratings. It is a show whose actors will always be  called by their character names. It is a show that has transcended boundaries and countries.”Sharing his fondest memories as Mr. Rathore, Gaurav further adds, “Playing the role of Raghuvendra Pratap Rathore has taught me that respect for an actor is a far bigger achievement than stardom. It has also taught me how to co-exist in a space where everyone is famous. It is a show that you feel proud of when you walked on the streets because people reacted to your presence with that kind of infectious excitement. My experience on the show has led me to believe that there will always be a section of the audience who will call me Mr. Rathore and I would like to thank COLORS for giving me this show as the perfect platform to connect with my fans.”

A show which is still creating conversations amongst its viewers, Uttaran, will draw to a close today. The show will surely be missed by not only the show’s fans, but also by the entire team that has strived hard to make this show television’s hottest property for the 6 years that it has aired on COLORS!

To watch Meethi and Mukta’s sojourn reach a happy ending, don’t miss Uttaran as it enters its last segment today only on COLORS!
